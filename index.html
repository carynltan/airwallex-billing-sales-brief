Product Overview

Airwallex Billing is an end-to-end quote-to-cash platform that enables businesses to create, send, and collect payment for invoices and subscriptions globally. Built on Airwallex's global payments infrastructure, Billing eliminates the need for third-party billing tools and removes FX friction for companies operating across borders.

Core Capabilities
  • One-off invoicing with 160+ payment methods
  • Subscription management (flat, tiered, multi-frequency)
  • Usage-based billing with meters & aggregation
  • Hosted Billing Checkout (low-code go-live)
  • Automatic tax calculation (sales tax, VAT, GST)
  • Smart payment retries & dunning
  • Credit notes, partial refunds, proration
  • 130+ currency support
  • Webhook-driven event notifications
  • Full REST API with sandbox environment
How It's Positioned
One Platform Advantage
Airwallex Billing is the only billing solution natively connected to a global payment account — meaning revenue collected flows directly into Airwallex, enabling FX conversion, global payouts, and spend management without a separate treasury layer.
Key Differentiator vs. Stripe Billing
Airwallex Billing is built for globally-minded businesses. Stripe requires separate international accounts; Airwallex supports multi-entity, multi-currency billing in a single integrated platform.

Pricing & Sales Process

Verified: Airwallex Billing has no separate product-level pricing tier — it is included as part of existing Airwallex account plans at no added cost. This was confirmed publicly by Airwallex's Chief Product Officer at launch (May 2026). Revenue for Airwallex is generated through payment processing fees on transactions collected through the Billing product.

Airwallex account plans (as at mid-2025, per airwallex.com/pricing): The platform uses a tiered subscription model — Explore (free, with qualifying deposit or balance conditions), Grow (mid-tier, monthly fee per legal entity), and Accelerate (enterprise, from ~£999/month). These tiers govern spend management features, not Billing specifically. Transaction-level fees for FX and payment processing apply on top of any plan tier.

Payment processing fees (indicative, varies by region and plan): Domestic card processing ~2.8% + $0.30; international cards ~4.3% + $0.30. FX markup: 0.5% for major currencies (USD, EUR, GBP, SGD, HKD, AUD), up to 1.0% for other currencies. These apply to payments collected through Billing. Always confirm current rates with the Commercial/Pricing team — rates are region- and volume-dependent.

For deal-specific pricing, refer to the [Internal Pricing Guide] or work with your Commercial/Pricing team. Volume discounts and custom enterprise pricing are available.

Frequently Asked Questions
Does Airwallex Billing work without an existing Airwallex payment account?
No. Airwallex Billing is natively connected to Airwallex's payment infrastructure. Merchants must have an active Airwallex account to collect payments through Billing. This is also a key selling point — revenue settles directly into the same account used for FX, payouts, and cards.
What billing models are supported?
Airwallex Billing supports flat-rate, per-unit, tiered pricing, multi-frequency subscriptions, usage-based billing (via meters and usage events), one-off invoices, and hybrid models combining multiple billing types in a single subscription.
How does Automatic Tax Calculation work? Does it file returns?
Airwallex Tax automatically calculates indirect tax (VAT, GST, sales tax) on invoices, subscriptions, and checkout flows based on the merchant's tax registrations, product tax categories, and customer billing address. It does not file returns or register the merchant with tax authorities — those remain the merchant's responsibility. Exportable CSV tax reports support the filing workflow.
How long does integration typically take?
Hosted Billing Checkout can be integrated in a matter of days with minimal code. A full custom integration using the Native Payments API may take 4–8 weeks depending on engineering bandwidth and complexity of billing models.
Can a customer migrate from Stripe or Chargebee?
Yes, though migration complexity varies. Key considerations include migrating existing customer records, payment sources (stored cards), active subscriptions, and historical invoice data. Work with the SE team to scope a migration plan. Airwallex does not provide an automated migration tool at this time.
Is there a no-code / zero-code option for non-technical teams?
Yes. The Airwallex Web App allows finance and ops teams to create invoices, set up subscriptions, configure pricing, and manage billing customers without writing code. Hosted Billing Checkout requires minimal backend code from a developer, but the customer-facing experience is fully managed by Airwallex.
What happens when a subscription payment fails?
Airwallex Billing automatically retries failed payments using a smart retry schedule. The subscription moves to UNPAID status. Automated email reminders are sent to the customer. If payment is eventually collected, the subscription returns to ACTIVE. If not, it moves to CANCELLED. Merchants can also configure custom dunning flows via webhooks.
Is usage-based billing (meters) generally available?
Usage-based billing is available via API. Full Web App support for meters is on the roadmap — confirm current status with the Product team before committing to prospects. Flag as a near-term GA capability when relevant.
